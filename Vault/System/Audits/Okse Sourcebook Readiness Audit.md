# Okse Dominion — Sourcebook Readiness Audit

This is a creator-only editorial audit of the current CITADEL Okse material against a hypothetical commercial regional sourcebook.

It is **not** a canon article, completion percentage or automatic task list.

## Hypothetical product scope

> **The Okse Dominion — a 60–100 page CITADEL regional sourcebook**

The test is whether an editor or writer could recover the foundational world facts needed to build that product without first reverse-engineering how the Dominion functions.

## Strong

### Regional identity and physical character

The core article already establishes a distinctive mountainous/highland environment, harsh weather, defensive geography and a strong sensory identity. The setting does not read as a generic northern kingdom with renamed places.

### Culture and social hierarchy

Oksmun values, clothing, warrior culture, Thralls, Ambátts, Leysingi and the wider dominance/submission structure provide strong material for describing how power appears in ordinary interactions.

### Economy and material identity

Oil, mining, metalworking, arms manufacture, structural engineering and slave-supported industry are unusually well connected. Oil affects labour, lighting, military technology, shipping and political economy rather than existing as a detached export entry.

### Resonance

The distinction between regulated Muld Resonants and more permissive Svar use gives the Dominion a clear relationship with a setting-defining VISCERIUM concept.

### Warfare

The Dominion has a recognisable military identity: large standing forces, darkened armour, tower shields, Juggernauts and limited strategic Resonant deployment.

### Named internal geography

The article identifies Valenheim, Rauthrbak Min, Hjalliberg, Aldaness, Vagrvik and Strondverdir. This is enough to establish that the Dominion contains more than one anonymous capital and surrounding wilderness.

## Thin

### Settlements below the national level

The named holdings currently function mostly as names. A regional book would need at least a few of them to establish why they exist, what distinguishes them, who controls daily life there and what a visitor can actually do or encounter.

This does **not** mean all six require equally large articles.

### Government below named rulers

The article establishes major rulers and a strong social order, but the practical machinery between the ruling family and an ordinary resident remains thin. Local officials, Jarls, courts, tax collection, property authority or comparable mechanisms only need definition where they materially shape stories.

### Religion, ritual and belief

Bone charms, runes, sacrifice, ancestral ideas and old prayers create strong atmosphere, but their institutions and everyday consequences remain comparatively unclear. A sourcebook writer could describe the aesthetic but would still have to decide who conducts rites, what authority they possess and what ordinary observance looks like.

### Travel and infrastructure

Terrain and maritime movement are established, and longboats participate in trade, but movement between the named holdings is not yet concrete. Roads, passes, rivers, ports, seasonal isolation and journey expectations would provide substantial regional value.

### Everyday civilian institutions

Work and hierarchy are strong, but medicine, hospitality, markets, childhood/training, dispute resolution and leisure remain relatively light. Only distinctive elements need canon; generic medieval assumptions can remain assumptions.

### Local ecology as linked entities

The article describes mammoths, highland cattle, Sigdfugl, deer, wolves, bears, foxes, marine life, berries and fungi. The sourcebook value will increase when distinctive VISCERIUM organisms are independently authored and linked to the Dominion rather than existing only as wildlife prose.

## Missing but valuable

These are gaps worth considering because filling them would reduce foundational invention for the hypothetical product.

### A small settlement network

Develop **two or three** contrasting holdings rather than all named places at once. A useful trial would include:

- the capital or major political centre;
- an industrial/mining settlement;
- a coastal, frontier or otherwise contrasting community.

That is enough to test whether the location workflow works at different scales.

### One or two meaningful routes

Establish at least one route that explains how people, ore, oil, soldiers or slaves move between important places. The point is not a road atlas; it is to make geography operational.

### Practical authority

Clarify one representative chain of authority below the ruling family: who can arrest someone, collect dues, control a mine, settle a dispute or close a road.

### Ordinary ritual

Establish at least one common Oksmun religious/ancestral practice in enough detail that a writer can depict it without inventing the underlying belief system.

## Safe to invent later

These do not need to become Codex canon merely to make a future product possible:

- encounter-table probabilities;
- RPG statistics or challenge ratings;
- exact prices for routine goods;
- exhaustive population figures;
- room-by-room settlement maps;
- random name tables;
- adventure hooks written specifically for one rules system;
- exact travel times for every possible route;
- exhaustive inventories of shops and services.

A product writer can create these from established canon without changing what VISCERIUM fundamentally is.

## Authoring-system findings

The audit supports the new authoring direction:

1. **Location depth is the clearest structural weakness.** The national article already names places but does not yet give them reusable local identities.
2. **Everyday-world prompts belong mostly in guidance, not compulsory YAML.** Okse already answers many of them naturally in prose.
3. **Storyteller fields are valuable for factions and locations.** The existing article contains strong facts, but concise present-tense handles such as current pressures, reach, local tensions and first impressions would make them easier to reuse.
4. **No publication-builder feature is required.** The deficit is underlying local canon, not PDF assembly tooling.
5. **Do not expand the schema again until real Okse locations use the current modules.** The next useful work is content population and feedback, not more fields.

## Recommended next content trial

Create or develop three contrasting Okse location notes using the progressive location workflow:

1. **Valenheim** — major settlement / political centre.
2. **Rauthrbak Min** — industrial/prison site with economic and coercive significance.
3. **One contrasting coastal, frontier or secondary settlement** from Hjalliberg, Aldaness, Vagrvik or Strondverdir once its role is established.

After those three notes exist, review which location fields were genuinely useful and remove or consolidate any that encouraged filler.

Do not create all three merely to satisfy this audit. They become tasks only when deliberately chosen as current worldbuilding work.
