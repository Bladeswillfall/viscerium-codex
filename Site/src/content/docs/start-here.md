VISCERIUM is one continuous history told across four eras. Kingdoms become republics, ritual becomes science, and humanity's wars eventually escape the planet—but the old forces beneath civilisation never disappear.

The setting begins in the **Degel System**, centred on the world of **Errack**. It is a place of severe beauty, hostile frontiers and recurring incursions, where survival is rarely an individual achievement and every solution leaves something behind for the next age.

## Choose an era

- [**CITADEL**](/eras/citadel/) — steel, bone, early gunpowder and Resonance understood through ritual.
- [**SMOG**](/eras/smog/) — industrial expansion, trench warfare and occult machinery.
- [**NEARSIGHT**](/eras/nearsight/) — satellites, exoskeletons and cassette-futurist warfare in a world that still cannot see the whole threat.
- [**ENTROPY**](/eras/entropy/) — orbital conflict, altered flesh and the revelation of what surrounds the Degel System.

## What connects the eras

**Resonance** is the setting's central supernatural force: a means of affecting matter and reality through harmonics, will and physical conduits. Its interpretation changes across history, but its consequences accumulate.

**Warfare** shapes technology, borders and ordinary life. VISCERIUM's largest threats demand collective action; lone heroes can change a moment, but nations and cultures determine whether anyone survives it.

**Culture** keeps the world human. Belief, folklore, trade, grief and ambition persist beneath changing weapons and institutions. Every era inherits the stories—and the mistakes—of the one before it.
